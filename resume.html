Recent Experience:

Archivist at Culver's / May 2015 to Present


Founded in 1984, Culver's is a family-oriented, Midwestern-based, 'fast-casual restaurant,' which operates over 550 locations in twenty-two states, and employs thousands. Since joining Culver's as the organization's first and only archivist, I: Developed and enacted a mission statement for the archive, which received approval from Culver's stakeholders, and outlines what the archive exists to document, collect, and preserve; Completed a full, item-level inventory of the Culver's collection, which amounts to approximately five-hundred cubic feet and contains audiovisual, paper-based, and three-dimensional materials; Assisted with the development of a digital media library — called 'Culver's in the News' — where Web clippings, videos, and presentations concerning Culver's are cataloged, uploaded, and stored for use by employees. In charge of cataloging each resource, I developed and maintain a Culver's-specific controlled vocabulary, and upload each piece of media to the SharePoint portal with relevant metadata adhering to Dublin Core standards; Directed the digitization of the organization's tape-based media, which amounted to one-hundred and twenty-two U-Matic, Digital Betacam, and VHS tapes. In support of the project, I prioritized digitization based on both deterioration and eventual use of our material, developed a two-year budget, wrote a request for proposal (which was sent to vendors across the country), and selected a vendor.

Web Archiving Intern at the Library of Congress / June 2015 to July 2015


In support of the Library's Office of Strategic Initiatives, I: Analyzed the Library's crawl of loc.gov and loc.gov/staff, to uncover ineffective and unnecessary crawls; Compiled contact information for Websites in the process of being crawled; and edited the permission letters sent out on behalf of the Library before a site is crawled.

Project Assistant at the Wisconsin Historical Society / January 2014 to September 2015


In support of the Wisconsin Historical Records Advisory Board, I: Wrote email and mail communication on behalf of the State Archivist concerning archival events, advocacy, and outreach, most of which was sent to over one-thousand institutions across Wisconsin on a weekly basis; Assisted with the planning of Archives Month, including the design of the Archives Month poster, and communication regarding event planning and publicity; Served as a Wisconsin Historical Society liaison to the Board and oversaw travel logistics, purchases, and communication on behalf of board members; and co-wrote a National Historical Publications and Records Commission Board Programming Grant application to serve the Board's future endeavors. The Grant was successful, and the Board was awarded $24,000.
